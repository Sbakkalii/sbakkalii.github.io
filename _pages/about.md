My Background
------
I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science in 2022 from [La Rochelle Université](https://www.univ-larochelle.fr/), focusing on Computer Vision (CV) and Natural Language Processing (NLP). My [thesis](https://theses.hal.science/tel-04197696v1/document) proposed multimodal document understanding frameworks that unify vision and language in a cross-modal learning setting. I obtained an engineering degree (M.Eng) in Information Technology from [INPT](http://www.inpt.ac.ma/) in Morocco.

Current Role
------
I am an Enseignant-Chercheur Contractuel and Research Scientist at IRISA-Lab, ESIR, Université de Rennes, within the SHADOC team (Rennes, France). Previously, I was an Associate Professor at L3i-Lab, La Rochelle Université (2023-2025), and a postdoctoral researcher and lecturer in 2023.

Research Interests
------
My work centers on multimodal AI for document understanding, including vision-language models, document classification and retrieval, abstractive summarization, and low-resource scene-text recognition. I also work on explainable multimodal systems, privacy-aware learning (federated approaches), and reliable RAG pipelines for knowledge-grounded document applications.

Selected Impact
------
I have authored 20 peer-reviewed publications (223 citations, H-index 6) and secured 432,000+ EUR in competitive funding as PI/Co-PI. I currently co-supervise PhD students and contribute to international collaborations across Europe and Asia.
